Simulated Annealing

Simulated Annealing (We will use SA for short) is a method used for solving unconstrained and bound-constrained optimization problems. For this particular project, we use the hill climbing technique to improve the current solution. SA models the process of heating, then slowly lowering the temperature as the energy lowers. At each iteration of the SA algorithm, a new point is randomly generated. The algorithm accepts all new points that lower the energy, and also within a certain probability raises the energy. By accepting points that raise the energy, SA is able to find more possible solutions. A cooling algorithm will decrease the temperature as the algorithm processes. While the temperature cools, SA reduces its search to reach a minimum energy.


Genetic Algorithms

Genetic Algorithms (We will use GAs for short) are a fairly new method to programing. It is derived from biological evolution. They are most commonly used to generate high quality solutions for search and optimization problems. GAs simulate natural selection, in which "survival of the fittest" among individuals applies. Each new generation consists of a population of individuals, and each individual represents a point in search space, and a possible solution. A string of variables represents each individual. This string is comparable to a chromosome.
